# Nairobi Chamber of Commerce — Site Plan

## Site Name
**Nairobi Chamber of Commerce**

## Site Purpose
This website will serve as a resource hub for businesses operating in Nairobi, Kenya. The site will provide information on local events, host networking opportunities, maintain a searchable business directory, and encourage residents and visitors to shop and invest locally. It aims to promote economic growth across Nairobi's business community, support small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and foster a stronger sense of collaboration between businesses, entrepreneurs, and residents.

## Target Market
Business owners, entrepreneurs, corporate leaders, and patrons within Nairobi and its surrounding areas (e.g., Westlands, Kilimani, Eastleigh, Karen, Kasarani).

## Site Goals
- Improve member engagement by providing valuable resources, mentorship opportunities, and networking events.
- Attract new businesses and encourage investment and economic development within Nairobi.
- Strengthen the Chamber's visibility and reputation as a trusted authority on local business matters.
- Support SMEs and informal traders (jua kali sector) in accessing formal business networks and resources.

## User Personas

**Small Business Owner — Wanjiru Kamau**
Wanjiru is a 34-year-old entrepreneur who owns a boutique fashion shop in Kilimani. She is looking for networking opportunities, access to business grants, and marketing support to help grow her brand and connect with suppliers.

**Corporate Executive — Otieno Odhiambo**
Otieno is a 45-year-old executive at a logistics firm based in Industrial Area. His company is interested in partnership opportunities, economic development resources, and networking with other business leaders to explore expansion across East Africa.

**The New Resident — Wafula Simiyu**
Wafula recently relocated to Nairobi from Bungoma for work. He is looking to learn more about the local business environment, find reputable service providers, and get involved in community initiatives. He visits the site for the business directory and upcoming community events.

## Scenarios
- A local business owner, like Wanjiru, is interested in joining the Chamber to network with other entrepreneurs. She visits the website to find information on membership benefits, fees, and the application process.
- A resident is looking for upcoming business workshops or networking mixers hosted by the Chamber. He visits the website to browse the events calendar and register for sessions on topics like digital marketing or access to finance.
- A visitor considering relocating to Nairobi, like Wafula, wants to understand the local business environment. He explores the Chamber's website to review the business directory, learn about the availability of skilled labor, and get a sense of the city's economic landscape.

## SEO Plan
- Use relevant keywords such as "Nairobi business directory," "Nairobi Chamber of Commerce," and "Nairobi networking events" in site descriptions, page content, and blog posts.
- Verify the site on Google Business Profile.
- Encourage member businesses to link back to the Chamber site to build inbound backlinks and improve domain authority.
- Embed Google Analytics across all site pages to track visitor behavior and optimize content.

## Design Brief

|---|---|---|
| Primary Color | `#0A6E4B` (Kenyan flag green) | Represents growth, land, and prosperity |
| Secondary Color | `#C8102E` (Kenyan flag red) | Bold accent for CTAs and highlights |
| Background Color | `#F7F5F0` (warm off-white) | Clean, neutral backdrop |
| Text Color | `#1A1A1A` (near-black) | High readability/contrast |
| Font Family | `"Poppins", sans-serif` for headings; `"Inter", sans-serif` for body text | Modern, clean, highly legible across devices |

*(Feel free to adjust these to your own taste — this gives a cohesive East African identity while staying professional.)*

**Directory Page:**
- Page title: "Directory"
- View toggle: **Grid** / **List** buttons

  - Business Name
  - Business Tag Line

### Notes for Development
- Keep the site map's grayed-out pages (**About**, **Podcast**) as placeholders for future weeks if your course timeline builds them out later — mirror the "active vs. future" styling shown in the example (solid border for built pages, faded/gray for upcoming ones).
- Since this is a personal project (not a group directory), you can seed the initial Directory page with 6–9 real or realistic Nairobi-based businesses (e.g., a Kilimani boutique, an Eastleigh electronics shop, a Westlands tech startup, a Karen café) to make the layout feel authentic when you demo it.
- Consider using `.co.ke` domains in your placeholder URLs to reinforce the local Nairobi context (e.g., `mybusiness.co.ke`).
